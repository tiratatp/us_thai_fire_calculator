# Thai Personal Income Tax — Reference for Thai Tax Resident (≥180 days/yr)

## 1. PIT Progressive Brackets (2024/2025)

| Taxable Income (THB) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 150,000 | 0% |
| 150,001 – 300,000 | 5% |
| 300,001 – 500,000 | 10% |
| 500,001 – 750,000 | 15% |
| 750,001 – 1,000,000 | 20% |
| 1,000,001 – 2,000,000 | 25% |
| 2,000,001 – 4,000,000 | 30% |
| Over 4,000,000 | 35% |

**Source:** https://www.rd.go.th/english/6045 ; https://www.rd.go.th/fileadmin/download/english_form/2024/GUIDE_90_67_Complete.pdf

Note: NOTE the top brackets differ from what I initially assumed. Confirmed brackets are 2M-4M @ 30% and >4M @ 35% (not 2M-5M / >5M).

## 3. Foreign-Sourced Income Remittance (Por 161/2566, Paw 162/2566)

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Por 161/2566** (Sep 15 2023, effective Jan 1 2024) | Foreign income (§40(1)-(8)) taxable in the year remitted, regardless of when earned |
| **Paw 162/2566** (Nov 20 2023) | Grandfathering: income earned **before Jan 1 2024** is NOT taxable when remitted (even if remitted 2024+) |
| **2025 status** | No legislative change as of early 2026 |
| **DTA relief** | Foreign tax credit available under DTAs (US-Thailand treaty applies) |

**Source:** https://www.hlbthai.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/RD-Instruction-No.-Paw161-2566-Translation.pdf ; https://kpmg.com/xx/en/our-insights/gms-flash-alert/flash-alert-2023-238.html ; https://www.expattaxthailand.com/thailand-revenue-department-foreign-sourced-income/

## 4. Treatment of US Account Remittances

| Source | Thai Treatment |
|---|---|
| Traditional 401(k)/IRA | Pension income §40(1). Fully taxable when remitted. FTC via US-Thai DTA |
| **Roth IRA/401(k)** | **Thailand does NOT recognize Roth**. Entire withdrawal taxable as pension income when remitted |
| Taxable brokerage | **Gain portion only** taxable; principal (basis) not assessable. Requires basis records |
| Return of principal | Not taxable — principal is capital, not income |
| **Pre-2024 savings** | **Exempt** under Paw 162/2566 (regardless of remittance date) |

**Source:** https://www.expattaxthailand.com/your-questions-answered/ ; https://www.expattaxthailand.com/thailand-revenue-department-foreign-sourced-income/

**CRITICAL for algorithm design:** Roth is NOT protected in Thailand. This changes the "Roth last" heuristic substantially.

## 5. Thai Capital Gains

| Asset | Treatment |
|---|---|
| SET-listed shares | Exempt for individuals |
| ASEAN-Link shares | Exempt |
| Mutual fund units | Exempt (Royal Decree 689/2562) |
| Unlisted shares / OTC | Fully taxable at PIT rates |
| Digital assets (SEC-licensed platform) | Exempt 2025-2029 |

**Source:** https://www.set.or.th/en/market/information/tax

## 6. FX Conversion

- No separate FX gain tax.
- THB equivalent of remitted foreign income at time-of-remittance rate is assessable.
- Distinguish principal (not assessable) from income/gains (assessable). Records required.

**Source:** RD Q&A via https://www.expattaxthailand.com/thailand-revenue-department-foreign-sourced-income/

## 7. Filing

| Form | Who | Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| PND 91 | Employment income only | Paper Mar 31, e-file Apr 8 |
| **PND 90** | Employment + other (including **foreign remittances**) | Paper Mar 31, e-file Apr 8 |
| PND 93 | Mid-year withholding adjust | Sep 30 |

Filing threshold: gross assessable >120k THB (or >220k combined married).

**Source:** https://www.rd.go.th/fileadmin/download/english_form/2024/GUIDE_90_67_Complete.pdf ; https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/thailand/individual/tax-administration
